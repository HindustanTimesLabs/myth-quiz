From JNU to Ramjas, can you separate fact from fiction

By Prawesh Lama, Samarth Bansal and Gurman Bhatia

February 25, 2017

Teachers, students and journalists were hurt on the Delhi University campus when student groups clashed over the disruption of a literary festival in Ramjas College.

Ramjas college’s Literary Society was hosting a seminar that it was forced to call off after members of BJP’s student wing, Akhil Bharatiya Vidyarthi Parishad (ABVP), kicked up a fuss. Their reason: JNU students Umar Khalid and Shehla Rashid were among the speakers.

ABVP says it’s “anti-national” to invite Khalid, who was arrested in 2016 over sedition charges. He was organising a rally on the JNU campus in which anti-India slogans were allegedly heard. Rashid was then the JNU student union vice president.
